💰 Financial Independence

Building Financial Independence

Financial control is one of the most common tools of abuse. These steps help you build independence — safely, quietly, and with legal backing.

🏦 Opening Your Own Bank Account+

You have the absolute right to open a bank account in your name — without your partner's knowledge or permission.

  • PM Jan Dhan Yojana: Open a zero-balance savings account at any nationalised bank with just your Aadhaar card. No minimum balance required. Free RuPay debit card included.
  • Regular savings account: Bring Aadhaar + PAN card to any bank branch. You do not need a spouse's signature or consent.
  • Give your parents' address or a trusted friend's address as your account address — not your matrimonial home.
  • Opt for paperless/e-statements only — no physical statements that could be intercepted.
  • Set up a separate mobile number (a new SIM) for bank OTPs.
✅ Start small: Transfer or save small amounts of cash to your new account regularly. Even ₹200 a week becomes a meaningful safety cushion over time.
💛 Claiming Your Stridhan+

Stridhan is legally yours — completely and absolutely. No court in India can award it to your spouse or partner.

Steps to document and reclaim your Stridhan

  1. Make a written inventory — list every item of jewelry, gifts, and cash you received before, during, and after the wedding. Include approximate value.
  2. Gather evidence — old photographs from the wedding, gift receipts, family witness statements, or a list signed by your parents.
  3. If your Stridhan is being held by in-laws: File a case under Section 406 IPC (Criminal Breach of Trust). The court can order its return.
  4. Include it in your PWDVA petition as "economic abuse" — the Magistrate can order its return as part of the Monetary Relief order.
✅ Free legal help: NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) provides free lawyers for this. Contact your District Legal Services Authority or call 181.
🏛️ Government Schemes for Women+

Direct financial support

  • Swadhar Greh: Government shelter homes provide free accommodation, food, vocational training, and legal aid for up to 2 years.
  • One Stop Centre (Sakhi) Fund: OSCs can provide immediate financial assistance for transport, medicines, and basic needs — ask at your nearest government hospital.
  • State-specific schemes: Most states have distressed women's funds. Ask the Protection Officer or 181 about schemes in your state.

Employment and skill building

  • PM Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY): Free skill training in 300+ sectors. Certificates that are recognised nationally.
  • Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Grameen Kaushalya Yojana (DDU-GKY): Placement-linked skill training for rural women.
  • Self Help Groups (SHGs): Connect with a local SHG through the State Rural Livelihood Mission. Access to small loans (₹10,000–₹1,00,000) at very low interest rates.
✅ The Protection Officer assigned to your PWDVA case can directly connect you to welfare schemes in your district. This is part of their official mandate — ask them explicitly.
📄 Securing Financial Documents+
  • Photograph all financial documents: bank passbooks, fixed deposit certificates, property papers, LIC policies, mutual fund statements, and joint investments.
  • Save these photographs to a private cloud folder the abuser cannot access.
  • Note down account numbers, policy numbers, and the names of banks/institutions.
  • If there are joint investments or FDs, consult a lawyer about how to prevent them from being liquidated without your consent.
  • Joint bank accounts: Contact the bank to understand the terms — most joint accounts allow either holder to withdraw. A lawyer can seek a court injunction to freeze the account if needed.
⚠️ Do this quietly: Photographing documents when the abuser is out or asleep. Do not remove originals without legal advice — this can complicate proceedings.
⚖️ Maintenance & Alimony Rights+

During separation (interim maintenance)

  • Under CrPC Section 125, a wife can apply for maintenance from her husband immediately — even before divorce proceedings begin. (Similar rights exist under other personal and gender-neutral family laws for survivors of other genders).
  • This can be filed at the Family Court or through the Magistrate's court. You can apply yourself without a lawyer (though one helps).
  • Interim maintenance orders typically take 60–90 days but can be obtained faster in domestic violence cases.

Under PWDVA

  • The Magistrate can order monetary relief — covering your rent, food, medical bills, and children's expenses — as part of the Protection Order proceedings.
  • This is in addition to, not instead of, maintenance under CrPC or Hindu Marriage Act.
✅ Free help: NALSA-empanelled lawyers can file maintenance applications for free if you qualify for legal aid (most DV survivors do). Contact your District Legal Services Authority.
